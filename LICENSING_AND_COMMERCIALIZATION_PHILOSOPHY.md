# Licensing & Commercialization Philosophy

## What We Believe

GiljoAI MCP is built in the open because we believe the best developer tools come from real collaboration. We want individual developers, tinkerers, and small teams to have full access to a powerful agent orchestration system without paying a dime or jumping through hoops.

At the same time, we need the right to build a sustainable business. When larger organizations deploy this software across teams, that is a commercial use case, and we want the option to monetize it.

These two goals are not in conflict. Here is how we balance them.

## Who Can Use This for Free

If you are a solo developer, you can use GiljoAI MCP for anything. Build products, sell services, run your business. There are zero restrictions on what you create with it. You get the full product, not a crippled "free tier."

The Community Edition is the real product. It is not a demo.

## When a License Is Needed

The only thing that triggers a licensing requirement is **multi-user deployment** -- meaning two or more user accounts in the system at the same time.

If you are a small startup with a few people and you want to use this together, reach out. We are not trying to squeeze money out of early-stage teams. Commercial licenses can be granted for free at our discretion. The point is that we know who is using the software in a multi-user capacity, not that everyone has to pay.

The licensing requirement exists so that when a large enterprise rolls this out across departments, we have the legal standing to charge for it. That is the business model.

## How the License Actually Works (No Legalese)

Our license (the **GiljoAI Community License v1.0**) boils down to these points:

1. **One user = completely free.** Use it, modify it, redistribute it, build commercial products with it. No restrictions.

2. **Two or more users = contact us.** You need a Commercial License. This might cost nothing (we can grant free licenses), or it might be a paid agreement depending on the scale.

3. **You can fork and modify the code.** You just have to keep the license file intact and not remove the edition branding. You also cannot claim the software is your own product.

4. **No warranty.** The software is provided as-is. We are not liable if something breaks.

5. **Break the rules, lose the license.** If you violate the terms, the license terminates.

## How Contributions Work

We welcome and encourage contributions. Bug fixes, features, documentation, all of it.

When you contribute code back to this repository (via pull request or patch), you are assigning the intellectual property rights of that contribution to GiljoAI LLC. In plain terms: you are donating the code to us.

Why? Because we ship this software under two license tracks -- the free Community License and paid Commercial Licenses. If we do not own the IP for every line of code, we legally cannot offer commercial licenses. This is standard practice for dual-licensed open source projects.

What you keep:
- **You can still use your own code** in your other projects. The assignment is about our copy in our repository, not about your ability to reuse your own work.

What you give up:
- **Any claim over how we use the contribution.** We might include it in a paid product, a SaaS deployment, or any other commercial offering.

If that does not work for you, no hard feelings. You are welcome to fork the repository and use it under the Community License terms without contributing back.

## The "Community Edition" Brand

The downloadable version of GiljoAI MCP is branded as the **Community Edition**. This is the full product. It is not limited, locked down, or missing features compared to what we run ourselves.

The branding exists so users know which edition they are running and so we can maintain a clear distinction between the open-source release and any commercial or hosted offering we might build in the future.
